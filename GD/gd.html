  • To clarify

  • Can you provide more insight into that
  • I didn’t quite get that, could you repeat it

  • Having discussed that, let’s shift to

  • What’s your take on this Name
  • I’d love to hear your thoughts on this
  • Does anyone else have a different opinion
  • What do you think about this Name

  • In conclusion
  • To quickly recap what we’ve discussed
  • To wrap up, the main takeaway is

  • In short
  • To simplify

  • There are pros and cons to both perspectives

  • It's essential to consider both sides of the issue

  • What if we tried
  • One way forward might be
  • In my opinion, we should
  • How about we
  • I think it would be beneficial to

  • That’s a great idea, a N a, me, and I think
  • I like the point Name made about
  • I agree with Name’s thoughts on
  • Building upon Name’s suggestion
  • I believe Name raises an important point

12 Acknowledging Another’s Point

  • I see your point and I agree with parts of it
  • I understand your view, and you bring up an important point
  • You’ve made a solid argument, and it’s something worth considering
  • That’s a valid perspective, and it’s something we should think about
  • You’ve raised a good point that we need to address

13 Countering an Argument

  • While I understand your point, I think
  • I see where you're coming from, but I believe that
  • That’s a reasonable argument, but there’s another side
  • Although that may be true, I think we should also consider
  • I disagree with that viewpoint because

  • That’s an interesting point, and it’s something to consider
  • I understand your point, though I would argue
  • We have different perspectives on this, but
  • I respect your point of view, but I feel that
  • I see your reasoning, and I’d like to offer a different take

15 Concluding the Discussion

  • After hearing everyone’s input, I believe
  • To conclude, it seems we can all agree that
  • Ultimately, we need to focus on
  • To sum up, we can conclude that

Ithu ennathu?

Ithu ellam discussion, meeting, group discussion, interview, debate time-la use panna koodiya standard English speaking phrases. Namakku enna sollanum-nu therinjaalum, professional-aa polite-aa solla words venum. Athukkuthaan ithu.

Yen use panrom?

1) Opinion clear-aa solla
2) Agree / disagree pannum pothu rude-a illama pesa
3) Example kuduthu support panna
4) Discussion smooth-aa continue panna
5) Meeting end-la proper-aa summarize panna

Ithu yellam padikanuma?

Illa. Yellathayum by heart padikka thevai illa.
Oru category-la irunthu one or two phrases unakku natural-aa varum-nu feel pannina pothum. Same meaning irukura sentences thaan, words mattum different.

Best practice enna?

Oru category-ku 1–2 sentences choose pannunga.
Daily speaking-la use pannunga.
Appuram slowly others add pannunga.
Fluency varum, confidence varum.

English sentence (one line):
These phrases help you express ideas clearly and politely in discussions and meetings.
